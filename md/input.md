### What I Learned
- They say ethics is about doing the right thing when no one is looking. **I think happiness is about smiling when no one is looking :-)**
  - I've done big trips across incredible landscapes and cool cultures. I've also had small moments of just reading an article I like in a cafe, appreciating flowers on a tree during a walk, or listening to a song I like in bed. I've noticed the big moments don't make me much happier than the small ones. And all else held equal, the small moments leave me less with a sense of ""what next?"" when they're done. I should probably make a list of small moments where I've felt happy and get more of those in my life :D
- I got this random invite to show up at a church in Tokyo to just meet anglophones that immigrated to Japan. I didn't really know any of the cultural context or what was going on. Though it's been so easy and fun to make friends by just going twice compared to spending SO long trying to talk to people in broken Japanaese over the last 3 months :D My friend Ocean invited me and knowing there was someone I could come back to talk to actually made me a lot more open to 'taking a risk' and wandering off to talk to strangers. It's odd because it's just in my mind - makes no tangible difference whether or not I have 'someone to check in with' AFTER I talk to new people. I **wonder if I can hack my brain into being more open to talking to strangers** with this. Ex: Calling a friend after meeting people even if the friend isn't there in person.
- Relatedly, the preacher at the church talked about this cliche idea I've been thinking about: a closed door just points to another open one. Stating it like that is trite. But going through life experiences where I observe being very unhappy about X just to have a great thing Y happen instead - that REALLY helps weather the ups and down of life. To share one such story, I was really angry at myself when I didn't get an internship at this one company I really liked, groq. I regretted it for weeks. 4 months later, all the people that succeeded at getting internships there had their offers rescinded last minute as groq was acquired by NVIDIA. Meanwhile I was enjoying my time in Japan. Thank goodness I didn't get the job!
