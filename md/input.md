### What I Learned
- Got very restless after travelling every weekend. It felt like I was just going places to go places. So shocked myself out of that by skipping a weekend trip and trying very new things - dog cafes, trying to join a debate league, and trying to meet more locals through language meetups. Good things, in moderation :-)
- Did my first trip with friends instead of solo travelling. Although there's more hassle, it's much nicer to have someone with you to appreciate the incredible things you see. 
- I really appreciate moments of stillness right now in life. At the end of my morning run, there's a Shinto temple with flags that flap in the wind. I'm usually mesmerised and just meditate/observe the flags for a while. Sometimes, it's easy to imagine that I'm no longer in the largest city in the world, but just in my own peaceful bubble for a moment. 
- I've been thinking - Japanese life is very optimised for calm and comfort. The trains are silent, people are polite, streets are clean, etc. On the surface, it seems great. Though when I kept living that way for a while, I missed the energy and excitement of other countries I've been to. Sometimes, it's nice to be snapped out of your bubble by a happy group of teens laughing way too loudly or blaring music from a street demonstration. It's the opposite of a monochromatic life.
